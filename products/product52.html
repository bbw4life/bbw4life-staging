How to Apply — 4 Steps to Perfect Nails
1
Prepare Your Nail Surface Clean nails thoroughly with a nail wipe or alcohol pad. Remove any oil, polish or residue. Dry completely — a clean surface is what makes the bond last.
2
Apply a Thin Layer of NailBond Glue Dispense a small amount of gel onto the nail or directly onto the tip. Less is more — a thin, even layer creates the strongest bond. Too much gel can weaken hold.
3
Press and Hold Firmly Place your nail tip, rhinestone or gem precisely. Press firmly for 10 to 15 seconds without shifting. For UV gel formula, cure under UV or LED lamp for 60 seconds for maximum bond strength.
4
Seal and Protect Apply a top coat over the area to seal the bond and add shine. Your nail art is now set to last days without lifting, shifting or breaking.
Queen Tip: Store your NailBond Glue capped tightly in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. This preserves the formula and ensures every application is as strong as the first.

Do I need a UV lamp to use NailBond Glue?
›

A UV or LED lamp is recommended for maximum hold but not strictly required. The gel will partially set on contact and continue to harden naturally over a few minutes. However, curing under a UV lamp for 60 seconds creates a significantly harder, more durable bond — especially for nail tips and heavier decorations. For rhinestones and small gems, the natural set is often sufficient. For full tip sets, we strongly recommend using a lamp.

Which size should I choose — 20g or 30g?
›

Choose the 20g (Metallic Pink) if you do nail art occasionally — for events, special occasions or when you want to refresh your look. It is compact and perfect for a starter kit or travel.

Choose the 30g (Light Pink) if you do nail art regularly, apply full tip sets, or simply want to have a solid supply at home. It offers the best value per application and means you will not run out mid-session.

Is this glue safe for natural nails?
›

Yes — the NailBond Glue is formulated to work on natural nail surfaces without causing damage when used correctly. The key is applying a thin layer and not forcing removal. To remove tips or decorations, soak in acetone-based nail remover and gently slide — never pull — to protect the natural nail underneath. At BBW4LIFE, beauty is about caring for yourself, not harming yourself.
