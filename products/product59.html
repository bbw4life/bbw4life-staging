How to Apply the TearOff Lip Gloss — 4 Steps
1
Prepare Your Lips Start with clean, dry lips. The peel-off formula adheres best to a smooth, clean surface.
2
Apply an Even Layer Apply a generous, even coat with the applicator. Stay within your lip line — a proper coat is key.
3
Wait for It to Set Allow 15 to 20 minutes to dry. Don't press lips together — let it set fully.
4
Peel and Reveal Gently peel from the corners inward. Smooth, vibrant colour — no smudging, no transfer.
Pro tip: Apply a thin clear balm underneath for extra moisture and smoother colour payoff.
Does the peel-off formula actually work or does it just smear when I try to remove it?
+

It works when you follow two simple rules: apply a generous, even coat and wait 15 to 20 minutes. Too thin or too early are the two most common mistakes. When done correctly, the film lifts cleanly — leaving smooth, vibrant colour with no residue, no smudging, no cup transfer.

How long does the colour last after peeling?
›

When applied correctly, colour lasts 4 to 8 hours. It stays significantly longer than traditional gloss because it bonds directly to lip skin. For maximum longevity, apply a thin base coat of clear balm 5 minutes before.

Is it safe for sensitive or dry lips?
›

Yes. For very dry lips, apply a thin clear balm underneath first. If you have known sensitivities to cosmetic dyes, do a small patch test on your inner wrist before full application.
